# An outline of the reporting template

## Title Page

Project Title:
Type of Report: (Baseline / Midline / Annual Progress / Final Evaluation / Learning Report)
Implementing Organization:
Donor:
Reporting Period:
Date:
Prepared by:

## Executive Summary

Provides a concise summary (1–2 pages) including:

- Purpose of the report
- Overview of the project
- Evaluation approach and methodology
- Key findings (quantitative & qualitative)
- Major conclusions
- Priority recommendations

## 1. Introduction
### 1.1 Background and Context

- Brief context of the sector and geographic area
- Relevant social, economic, environmental, political factors
- Project rationale

### 1.2 Project Description

- Theory of Change / Results Framework
- Target groups and intended beneficiaries
- Geographic coverage
- Core components or intervention areas

### 1.3 Purpose and Scope of the Evaluation / Report

- Evaluation objectives
- Evaluation criteria (e.g., OECD‑DAC: relevance, coherence, effectiveness, efficiency, impact, sustainability)
- Key evaluation questions

## 2. Methodology

### 2.1 Evaluation Design

- Evaluation type (e.g., rolling baseline, quasi‑experimental, cohort tracking)
- Data sources (quantitative, qualitative, secondary)
- Sampling approach & sample characteristics

### 2.2 Data Collection Tools & Processes

- Survey instruments
- KIIs, FGDs, observations
- Piloting and field procedures

### 2.3 Data Analysis

- Quantitative analysis methods (PSM, ANOVA, chi‑square, descriptive stats, indices)
- Qualitative analysis approach (coding, thematic analysis)
- Inflation adjustment, weighting, or data harmonization (if applicable)

### 2.4 Ethical Considerations

- Informed consent
- Confidentiality and data protection
- Vulnerable groups
- Limitations, risks & mitigation strategies


## 3. Respondent Profile

### 3.1 Demographics

- Age, gender, education, marital status
- Household characteristics
- MSME characteristics (sector, size, years of operation)

### 3.2 Socioeconomic Indicators

- Livelihood pattern
- Land, assets, and productive resources
- Business characteristics


## 4. Findings by Evaluation Criteria

### 4.1 Relevance

- Alignment with local needs & priorities
- Appropriateness of project design
- Targeting (gender, youth, vulnerable groups)

### 4.2 Coherence

- Synergies with government, NGOs, private sector
- Complementarity with other programs
- Internal coherence between activities and intended outcomes

### 4.3 Effectiveness

- Achievement of outputs and outcomes
- Analysis of performance indicators
- Successes, challenges, enabling and hindering factors

### 4.4 Efficiency

- Resource utilization
- Cost‑benefit / cost‑effectiveness analysis
- Timeliness and management systems

### 4.5 Impact

- Intended and unintended effects
- Long‑term income/employment effects (e.g., via PSM or trend analysis)
- Sector‑ or cohort‑specific differences
- Attribution and contribution analysis

### 4.6 Sustainability

- Institutional capacity
- Financial and market sustainability
- Behavioral and technical sustainability
- Exit or scale‑up prospects

## 5. Cross‑Cutting Themes
### 5.1 Gender Equality and Social Inclusion (GESI)

- Gender‑disaggregated results
- Inclusion of vulnerable groups
- Changes in empowerment, decision‑making, access to resources

### 5.2 Environment and Climate Sensitivity

- Adoption of sustainable practices
- Environmental risks and mitigation
- Climate adaptation contributions

### 5.3 Conflict Sensitivity & Shock Responsiveness

- Influence of conflict, displacement, or shocks
- Project adaptations in response to crises

## 6. Lessons Learned

- What worked well and why
- What did not work and why
- Unexpected findings
- Implications for MEL or program design

## 7. Conclusions
Provide a synthesis of evidence from all sections:

- Project performance
- Overall value and contribution
- Summary of progress toward outcomes

## 8. Recommendations
Provide clear, actionable recommendations, categorized by stakeholder:

- For donors
- For implementing organizations
- For government partners
- For communities / beneficiaries

Each recommendation included:

- Issue identified
- Action needed
- Priority level (High/Medium/Low)

## 9. Annexes

- Annex 1: Detailed methodology
- Annex 2: Survey tools (questionnaires, KII/FGD guides)
- Annex 3: Additional tables and figures
- Annex 4: Data dictionary and codebook
- Annex 5: Stata do‑file (GitHub repo)
Annex 6: Ethical approval documents
Annex 7: List of stakeholders consulted
