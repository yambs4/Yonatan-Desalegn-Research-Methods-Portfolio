# Redacted Methods Section

> Information is is redacted to ensure NDA compliance

The evaluation applied a convergent mixed‑methods design, integrating quantitative and qualitative data to assess changes in participation, inclusion, and household‑level outcomes.

---

## 1. Data Collection

Five digital tools were developed using XLSForm and deployed via Kobo Toolbox. These tools included structured household surveys, organizational assessments, and facilitated group discussions with community and governance actors.

Built‑in mechanisms ensured data integrity, including skip logic, constraints, enumerator IDs, and consistency checks.

---

## 2. Data Quality Assurance

Data quality procedures included:

- Daily submission monitoring
- Automated completeness checks
- Manual review of enumerator patterns
- Correction of inconsistent variables through a documented cleaning script

## 3. Analysis

Quantitative analyses were conducted in Stata and included:

- Summary statistics
- Cross‑tabulations and hypothesis testing
- Composite indicator construction
- Propensity Score Matching (PSM) to estimate intervention‑attributable change

Qualitative data were transcribed, coded, and thematically analyzed to complement quantitative findings.

## 4. Ethical Considerations

All tools and protocols adhered to informed‑consent standards. Personally identifiable information (PII) was excluded from reporting.
